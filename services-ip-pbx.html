Understanding

What is IP-PBX?

IP-PBX is a telecommunication device that acts as the central switching system for phone calls within a business working on an IP based infrastructure. It is used to oversee the outgoing and incoming calls across its telephone network.

Voice over Internet Protocol powers both SIP Trunking and IP-PBX Systems. An IP PBX server is less expensive, scalable and futuristic, letting a business take advantage of new communication features quickly.

Process

How Does an IP PBX Work?

Simple setup, powerful communication

01
Credentials Setup

We provide a set of credentials for one or more SIP trunking accounts, as required by your business needs.

02
Authentication

On authentication, incoming calls are presented to the PBX to be accepted and processed seamlessly.

03
Call Routing

The PBX functions independently to determine whether calls are handled internally or relayed to the PSTN/SIP Trunk.

04
Outgoing Calls

Once authenticated, users can reach an outside phone line by way of your SIP trunk automatically.
